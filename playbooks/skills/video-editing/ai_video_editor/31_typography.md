### 5.2. Text & title in SVG — make them POP

- **Content from `quote`** (the in-context anchor line). You may sharpen the wording (shorter / stronger) by your own judgment **unless the user dictated it**; if you change it, sync the `quote` field (`modify_scene` metadata) so the data stays consistent.
- **⭐ Copy correctness before style.** Before judging weight, glow, dead-zone, or layout, proofread every visible overlay string against the full script, `quote`, `talking_point`, `visual`, and domain context. A beautiful title with a typo, grammar issue, missing Vietnamese diacritic, wrong casing, wrong number/currency/%/symbol, wrong proper noun, wrong industry term, or awkward machine-written phrasing is a Gate 4/7 FAIL. Caption correctness does not prove overlay correctness; overlay text may be baked into an old spec or newly authored SVG.
- **⭐ Rendered image typo/grammar check — read what the screenshot shows.** Text can be correct in `quote`, `talking_point`, or source but wrong after rendering, especially when generated/image-baked visuals are involved. On every local-shown BEFORE/AFTER/final screenshot, read the exact visible title/label/value/chart/document/UI/generated-image text from the image itself. Pseudo-text, malformed glyphs, missing/wrong Vietnamese diacritics, stale old overlay text, wrong casing, wrong number/currency/%/symbol, typo, grammar issue, or wrong domain term = Gate 4/7 FAIL. For Vietnamese and other diacritic languages, inspect every mark, not just the base letters: tone marks, accents, horn/breve/circumflex, vowel marks, and `Đ/đ` must be present, placed correctly, and not swallowed by stroke/effects. The final screenshot must pass `Gate 7 RENDERED IMAGE TYPO/GRAMMAR CHECK`.
- **⭐ Readability is the floor, not the finish line.** For short-form video, a title must be graspable in the first second, not merely decipherable after staring. A title that is readable but thin, hollow-looking, grey, timid, or visually quiet is a FAIL. The title must be punchy: thick letter body, bright/vivid face colour, clear hierarchy, and enough visual energy to stop the scroll while still obeying copy, safe-zone, face, and caption gates.
- **⭐ Prominence is mandatory — whenever you (re)build a spec, the TITLE must be BIG, CLEAR, THICK-BODIED, and a BRIGHT/LIGHT color.** The title/headline is the **largest, boldest** element and uses a **bright color** (white or a bright accent) with a controlled dark outline/sticker edge — **never small, dark, dim, muted, thin-bodied, or over-stroked** (a dark/dim/thin title vanishes into the footage; an over-thick outline looks muddy and amateur). It IS the message — give it strong size/weight/color hierarchy above everything else. Because the overlay is **transparent over moving footage**, every text floats on the scene and must stay readable on ANY frame: give it **strong contrast + a controlled dark outline/shadow/scrim when needed**. For authored overlay text, the visible letter outline must stay thin: **0–2px max text stroke on the 720 canvas**. Do not make the visible face stroke so thick that cairosvg **swallows thin Vietnamese diacritic marks** (e.g. the hook on `Ỷ` rendered as a plain `Y`) or over-darkens text so it sinks — use open typography with shadow, repositioning, a loose local scrim/gradient, or non-container accent shapes instead. Never a thin, low-contrast line that sinks into the video, and never a brute-force black blob around the letters.
- **⭐ Large-title no-card rule — TITLE/HERO TEXT IS NOT A CARD.** Large text means the scene title/headline, primary CTA, hero quote, or hero number/metric (roughly title-sized or larger). It must render as **open typography**, not as text stuffed inside a rounded rectangle, chip, pill, card, black translucent box, panel, or banner. A card around large text is an aesthetic FAIL even if readable. If contrast is weak, fix it with 900-weight body, <=2px stroke, shadow/glow, better placement, localized scrim/gradient, underline/brackets, or a style-specific accent shape that does **not** read as a text container. Cards/chips are for secondary labels/values only, and only when they improve clarity.
- **⭐ Text stroke cap — VIỀN TEXT MỎNG, BODY CHỮ DÀY.** Authored SVG text stroke is a contrast separator, not a weight tool. **Any visible stroke/outline on a `<text>` element is capped at 2px on the 720×1280 canvas.** Secondary text should normally be **0px stroke**. This cap applies even to chart titles, illustration labels, callouts, badges, values, quote cards, and decorative display text; do not convert text to paths or duplicate back-stroke text just to fake a thicker outline. Shape/card/icon outlines are different and may be thicker when the design needs them.
- **Stroke/body split rule:** make a title strong by using a 900-equivalent font face, enough font size, a bright face fill, and a stacked face body. If the title still needs contrast, add a soft shadow, glow, scrim/gradient, sticker/accent shape, or improve placement — **do not keep thickening the text stroke**.
- **⭐ Mandatory title face stack — 8–15x, not optional.** In SVG, title/hero/primary CTA/hero metric text must be drawn as **8–15 same-fill face copies** with tiny offsets around the center, inside the same `data-wc-object` title group, then one final top copy with a **thin <=2px** stroke. Choose/pseudo-randomize the count per title/video so output does not look templated: 8–10 for very wide/condensed words, 11–14 for normal titles, 15 only when the screenshot still feels light. **This range is not an auto-pass.** The visual proof decides: if duplicate fill makes letters muddy/blobby, deforms the font, closes counters/negative space, swallows Vietnamese diacritics, crushes tracking, or looks less premium, reduce the count/offset or choose a sharper heavy font. **More than 15 face copies = FAIL.** Do not use one copy plus a stroke; that creates the thin outline-only look shown in the failure examples. Do not use a giant black back-stroke to fake boldness.
- **Approved title recipe (use this ratio when in doubt):** for a 60–70px Vietnamese hero title, use a Black/900 family, render the same text **12–15 times** in the same face color with small offsets around center (`x/y` radius roughly `1.5–3.5px`, including horizontal, vertical, and diagonal offsets), then put one final top copy with visible dark stroke about **1.5–2px**. Long lines may use 8–12 copies if space is tight; short or endpoint titles may use 14–15 only when the rendered screenshot keeps clean counters, clear diacritics, and crisp letterforms. If the screenshot needs more separation, use a non-text shadow/glow/scrim/accent shape or move the title to a quieter area; never exceed 15 face copies.
- **⭐ Semantic title gate — the title must be the scene's takeaway, not a decorative label.** It should answer "what should the viewer remember?" in one punchy line or two wrapped lines. Do **not** approve titles that merely repeat a panel label, duplicate a right/left caption, or state a vague noun phrase while the real lesson sits elsewhere. For comparison/diagram overlays, the title should name the rule or contrast (e.g. `MỖI CLAIM = MỘT DEDUCTIBLE`, `KHÔNG PHẢI MỖI KỲ`) while the panels carry the supporting labels.
- **Title thickness gate:** at 280×498, the title must read as a solid headline, not a caption. Use **900-equivalent typography** only: name a Black/ExtraBlack/900 family where possible (`Be Vietnam Pro Black`, `Inter Black`, `Montserrat Black`, `Anton` when VN glyphs render cleanly) and set `font-weight="900"` as well. Uppercase where possible, use the 8–15x face stack, and use either `solid_outlined` or a restrained `double_outline` from `styles/text_axes.md`. If the title looks hairline, hollow, washed out, weaker than panel labels, trapped inside a card, merely readable, muddy because the outline is too thick, or over-thick because duplicate fill has made the font blobby/deformed/diacritic-swallowing/negative-space-closing, Gate 4/6 fails even if the count is inside 8–15.
- **⭐ MANDATORY TITLE GATE PROOF — reading this module is not sufficient.** After drafting any title/hero line, the agent must explicitly print the master `Gate 4 TITLE GATE PROOF` before upload. The proof must name the exact title, the `quote`/`talking_point` words it encodes, the chosen 8–15x face-stack count, and PASS/FAIL the copy-correctness, semantic, hierarchy, first-second punch, thickness, overlay-preview check, and rendered preview text check when a preview exists. A skipped pre-upload preview is allowed when no cheap local renderer exists. After upload, the agent must also pass the composite screenshot title check and the rendered image typo/grammar check. If the agent cannot honestly write `PASS to upload` pre-upload and `PASS title`/`PASS rendered text` post-upload, it must revise the overlay/title first. This is a process gate, not a nice-to-have.
- **⭐ Secondary text/label gate — separate from title.** Non-title text includes chart values (`$500`), axis/bar labels (`Lần 1`), badges, card text, panel labels, callouts, captions inside the overlay, and helper/subtitle lines. These do **not** inherit the title's PASS. Before upload, inventory every non-title text string and pass the master `Gate 4 SECONDARY TEXT GATE PROOF`, including copy correctness and rendered preview text check when a preview exists; after upload, pass the `Gate 6 SECONDARY TEXT SCREENSHOT CHECK` plus `Gate 7 RENDERED IMAGE TYPO/GRAMMAR CHECK`. If any non-title text is misspelled, ungrammatical, domain-wrong, malformed, stale, or hard to read at 280×498, the overlay fails even when the title is perfect.
- **⭐ Small/non-title text must NOT have visible stroke/outline.** This is different from title text. A stroke that helps a 70px title usually makes 28–40px labels muddy, fat, and hard to read. For labels/values/card text, use a solid heavy font face, high-contrast fill, and real spacing. Prefer open text on a quiet area, or a subtle no-border backplate if it needs contrast. A chip/card/border is optional and should be used only when it improves clarity. If the label needs a border to survive, do **not** add a stroke; instead enlarge the label, simplify the wording, move it, or add a proper background shape with padding.
- **⭐ Text-container padding gate — readable is not enough.** Any label/value/card text inside a chip, pill, badge, bar, table cell, or panel must have generous interior padding. Target at least **24–32px horizontal** and **14–20px vertical** empty space around the rendered text bbox on the 720 canvas; visually, the 280×498 screenshot must show real breathing room. If a word touches or grazes a border, sits on top of a stroke, gets clipped by a rounded corner, or feels squeezed against the container, Gate 4/6 fails even if the letters are legible. First consider removing the unnecessary border/container and letting the text breathe; otherwise fix by widening the container, shortening the wording, splitting the text into two lines, or reducing only secondary text slightly while staying above the mobile-readable floor.
- **Title candidate discipline:** before committing, mentally test at least two title phrasings and choose the one that best carries the scene's rule. Bad: a noun label (`MỖI LẦN CLAIM`) when that same phrase already appears in a panel. Better: a rule (`MỖI CLAIM = MỘT DEDUCTIBLE`, `TRẢ THEO CLAIM, KHÔNG THEO KỲ`). The final title should reduce the viewer's confusion in one glance.
- **⭐ FONT — derive it PER VIDEO and VARY between videos (NEVER one fixed font — 10k users must not all look the same).** Pick ONE typeface for the whole video from its **brand preset → topic vibe → deliberate rotation**; keep it across every scene of that video, change it for the next (same discipline as the accent colour). For **Vietnamese** content pick from the **VN-covering families in the renderer set** (most display fonts tofu the diacritics): **Be Vietnam Pro · Inter · Montserrat · Roboto · Noto Sans · Source Sans 3 · Open Sans** (clean sans), **Anton · Roboto Condensed · Noto Sans Condensed** (display/condensed), **DejaVu Serif** (serif). English content → any family in the set. (The renderer ships 300+ VN-covering fonts; rotate genuinely.)
- **⭐ WEIGHT — title/hero text is 900 minimum; name the HEAVY variant of YOUR chosen font: `font-family="<Family> Black"` (or `…ExtraBlack` / `…900`) and set `font-weight="900"`. Recurring fix: stop making text thin.** **cairosvg IGNORES numeric `font-weight`** (cairo toy API = normal/bold only) and each weight is a **separate family name**, so `font-family="Montserrat"` (or any family) always renders the **thin Regular** no matter the weight. Name the heavy face: `Montserrat Black`, `Roboto Black`, `Be Vietnam Pro Black`, `Inter Black`… (svg2spec also **auto-maps** a heavy `font-weight` attribute → `<your family> <weight>`, so include `font-weight="900"` too.) Single-weight display fonts (e.g. **Anton**) are already heavy — don't add a weight suffix. Proof: a Black face renders **~1.5× the ink** of Regular. The dark stroke is ONLY a thin footage separator (**max 2px on the 720 canvas**) — **never crank the stroke or put the title in a card to fake boldness**.
- **⭐ FONT SIZE — ONLY the TITLE is pinned; every other text just has to be readable on a phone.** **TITLE: 60–96px, 900-equivalent / Black** — the one hard size+weight rule. Draw it as large as fits inside the 5% side margin; if a long title will not fit at 60px, **wrap it to 2 lines — never shrink the title below 60px and never put it in a card to make it feel safe**. **Everything else (eyebrow / label / sub / value) is the agent's free choice** of size, colour, and hierarchy — do NOT prescribe sizes for them. The ONLY hard rule there is a **MOBILE-READABLE FLOOR: no overlay text below ~30px on the 720-wide canvas** (a 25px line is unreadable on a phone — verified: the eyebrow "KHI CÓ TỔN THẤT" at 25px was far too small). Make secondary text secondary by **colour / weight / CAPS / a chip**, not by shrinking it below the floor and not by adding an outline. And any secondary text must **fit its CONTAINER** when a container is used — if a line spills past its card/chip edge, widen the container, shorten the text, or wrap (never let a label/sub overrun its card border). Before upload, estimate the rendered width ≈ chars × ~0.55 × font-px for a heavy sans (CAPS / Black runs wider, ~0.6) and keep each line inside the safe box `x∈[36,684]`; if a cheap local overlay preview is available, use it as a sanity check, but final ground truth is the post-upload screenshot (§5.5). **One exception to the title cap:** a `single_metric` **hero number** is a focal data point, NOT a title — draw it intentionally huge (~50–60% of canvas height, well above 96px), still as open typography, not inside a card.
- **Highlight 1–2 keywords — actually DO this when a word carries the message** (don't ship a flat one-colour block): make the key word(s) stand out with size, colour accent, underline, slash/bracket, glow, or heavier-still weight. For title/hero text, do **not** use a pill/card behind the words; keep it open typography. Small pills are only for secondary labels/badges when they improve clarity.
- **Style — on-brand, varied BETWEEN videos, not one-mold.** A single look across thousands of users must be avoided.
  - **Borrow from the video itself**: read `overlay.caption.config` (font / `textColor` / `borderColor` / weight) + `globalSettings.presets` → reuse the same family + palette. Each brand differs → output varies on its own.
  - **Consistent WITHIN one video, varied BETWEEN**: one style per brand/topic/tone, kept across that video's scenes; a different video → a different style.
  - **Axes to rotate**: font · palette (from brand/topic) · layout (centered/left/stack/grid) · highlight method · accent · animation.
  - **⚠️ RESIST THE REFLEX ACCENT (the model's bias).** Don't reach for ONE accent (e.g. amber `#FF8A00`) on every overlay. **Derive** it: (1) the video's **brand preset**; (2) the **topic's vibe** (warning/danger→red; safety/finance→green; tech/surveillance→cyan-steel; luxury→gold/deep-purple; youthful→coral/pink); (3) **deliberate rotation** between videos. Each time check internally: "did I pick this for the topic, or out of inertia?"
- **Vietnamese coverage** is handled by Be Vietnam Pro — no tofu boxes. Keep diacritics intact; numbers/`%` unchanged.

### Diversify the text look — do NOT ship flat-only

After the rules above, choose a **look** for this video and reproduce it in SVG via the recipes in **`styles/text_axes.md`** (axes: structure · depth `flat/beveled/soft_drop` · fill `solid/gradient/metallic` · typeface · casing; + 19 palettes; + 142 curated presets with preview images). If you use preset preview images, save/build a local contact sheet and show it before making your own selection; do not inspect preview PNGs privately and do not ask the user to pick a style. Derive one look per video from brand/topic/rotation, keep it across the video, change it next video. The TITLE may carry the richest treatment (bevel/metallic/gradient); secondary text stays simpler so hierarchy holds.

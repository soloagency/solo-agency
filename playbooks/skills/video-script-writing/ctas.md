# CTA playbook (video)

One CTA per video, at the very end, ~**15–18 words**, **specific + actionable**,
with a reason to act now, **tone-matched** to the script. Never stack two CTAs —
a divided ask gets ignored. Pick the bank that fits the video's intent.

## Bank 1 · Direct / Action-oriented
Drive a single concrete next step. Best for how-to / tactical videos.
- "Try it on your next post and watch what the first hour does to your reach."
- "Open your settings right now and change that one field before you forget."
- "Save this, then do step one today — not tomorrow."

## Bank 2 · Community / Engagement
Invite a reply — boosts distribution on social platforms.
- "Comment the one tip you'll actually use — I read every reply."
- "Which of these surprised you? Tell me below."
- "Tag the person who needs to hear this."

## Bank 3 · Resource / Value
Point to a deeper payoff (a guide, tool, next video).
- "The full checklist is in the description — grab it before your next launch."
- "Want the template I used here? It's one link away."
- "Part two breaks down the hard case — it's on the channel now."

## Bank 4 · Soft / Consultative
Low-pressure, trust-building. Best for brand / thought-leadership.
- "If this changed how you see it, that's the whole point — take what's useful."
- "No pitch. Just try the first idea and notice the difference this week."
- "Follow if you want the boring-but-true version of this stuff."

## Bank 5 · Owner / Lead-gen — DEFAULT for practitioners
The narrator IS the professional (see *WHO IS SPEAKING* in `SKILL.md`) — the CTA
routes the viewer to THEIR next step, framed as an offer of help, not a plea.
The comment-keyword pattern works on every platform.
- "Comment QUOTE and I'll run your numbers against what you're paying now — free."
- "DM me the word CHECKLIST and I'll send the exact list I use with my own clients."
- "Send this to your spouse, then book the 15-minute review — link's in my bio."

## Rules
- **Route value back to the narrator.** On a practitioner's channel the CTA
  points at THEIR offer, resource, or review (comment keyword, DM, booking) —
  never at a generic "professional" they'd be handing the lead to.
- **Match the tone.** A calm explainer shouldn't end with a hype "BUY NOW".
- **One ask.** "Like, comment, share, follow, and subscribe" = zero action.
- **Give a reason.** "…before your next launch" / "…in the first hour" beats a
  bare "comment below."
- **Earn it.** A CTA only works if the body delivered value first.

## Anti-patterns (avoid)
- Generic "thanks for watching, don't forget to subscribe."
- **"Talk to your agent / ask a professional / consult your loan officer" when
  the narrator IS that professional** — you just handed their lead to a competitor.
- A CTA that asks for effort the value didn't justify.
- Multiple competing asks.
- Salesy urgency with no real reason ("act now!!!").

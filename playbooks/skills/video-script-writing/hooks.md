# Hook playbook (video)

The hook is the first 1–2 sentences (≈10–22 words) the narrator says. It decides
whether the viewer stays. Treat it as the highest-leverage line you'll write.

## The 3-Layer Hook Formula

Every strong hook stacks three jobs into one or two sentences:

1. **Identity trigger** — the viewer instantly thinks *"this is about ME."*
   Target by **situation**, not job title. ❌ "marketers" → ✅ "anyone who's
   rewritten the same email five times and still hit send nervous."
2. **Curiosity tension** — open a gap the brain *needs* to close. Forms:
   - Contradiction: *"X, but actually Y."*
   - Hidden cost: *"the thing quietly costing you ___."*
   - Wrong belief: *"you've been told ___ — it's backwards."*
   - Missed mechanism: *"the step everyone skips."*
3. **Reward signal** — hint the transformation. Show the **what**, hide the
   **how**. The viewer should feel: *"if I scroll, I lose an edge."*

## The hard rules
- **Length: 10–22 words.** Longer = the hook leaks attention before it lands.
- **Reveal ~30%, hide ~70%.** Never state the conclusion in the hook.
- **Loss-framing > curiosity.** "Interesting to know" loses to "costly to miss."
- **Specific > clever.** A number, a name, a concrete cost beats wordplay.
- **Banned phrases:** "secrets", "everything you need to know", "the complete
  guide", "in this video", "let's dive in", "stay tuned", and any salesy tone.

## Generate-5-then-score
Write **five** hook variants, score each, ship the winner:

| Criterion | Weight | Question |
|---|---|---|
| Must-watch urgency | ×3 | Does skipping feel like a loss? |
| Partial reward | ×3 | Does it promise value without revealing the answer? |
| Specificity | ×2 | Concrete number/name/cost vs vague? |
| Identity match | ×2 | Will the target instantly feel "this is me"? |

## 12 proven hook templates (with examples)
1. **[Number] + [hidden cause] + [implied fix exists]** — "30% of profit vanishes
   from one line item nobody audits — and it takes 10 minutes to fix."
2. **The mistake you don't know you're making** — "You're losing followers every
   time you post at the 'best time' everyone told you to use."
3. **X but actually Y (myth flip)** — "Drinking 8 glasses of water a day isn't the
   rule you think it is."
4. **The cost of inaction** — "Every month you delay this one setting, you pay for
   it twice."
5. **Insider contradiction** — "Pros do the exact opposite of what beginners are
   taught about ___."
6. **Stakes + countdown** — "You have about 90 seconds to fix the thing killing
   your open rates."
7. **"I wish I'd known"** — "If I'd known this one number, I'd have saved two years."
8. **Most people / few people** — "Almost nobody checks the one field that decides
   whether your video even gets shown."
9. **Before/after gap** — "How a 12-word change took this from 200 views to 40,000."
10. **Forbidden / don't** — "Don't post another reel until you've checked this one
    thing." (use sparingly; never clickbait-y)
11. **Pattern interrupt question** — "What if the reason it isn't working has
    nothing to do with effort?"
12. **Specific persona callout** — "If you've ever opened your analytics and felt
    your stomach drop — this is the fix."

## Before → after (rewrites)
- ❌ "Today I want to talk about email marketing tips." (setup, no stakes)
  ✅ "Three words in your subject line are quietly halving your open rate."
- ❌ "Here's everything you need to know about sleep." (banned + vague)
  ✅ "You're sleeping 8 hours and still tired because of the 20 minutes before
  bed."
- ❌ "The complete guide to saving money." (banned + generic)
  ✅ "The 'small' subscription that costs the average person $4,200 over a decade."

## Per-language note
Write the hook in the **target language** naturally (don't translate word-for-
word — adapt idioms). For Vietnamese, keep it punchy and conversational; the same
3-layer logic applies. Keyword/visual search terms downstream are English, but the
spoken hook is in the content language.

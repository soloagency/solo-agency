# goal_type → email structure

The campaign's `goal_type` picks the shape. All are short + plain-text; the difference is the
CTA and where the value lands.

| goal_type | Structure | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| `book_meeting` | 1 load-bearing hook → 1 evidenced value line → offer a specific, tiny time ("15 min Thu?"). No links. | one time-bound ask |
| `get_reply` | hook → a genuine, specific **question** they'd want to answer. No offer, no link. | ends on the question |
| `direct_sale` | hook → value → **one** offer link (the only place a click-tracked link belongs, later). | "take a look: <one link>" |
| `reactivation` | reference the **prior relationship with evidence** ("last year you …") → "still doing X?" → light offer. | low-key re-open |
| `nurture_upsell` | for existing customers: a useful insight first, then a soft next-step tied to what they already bought. | soft, value-first |
| `event_invite` | why THIS person → what they get → date/RSVP. | RSVP |
| **final step (any)** | breakup: acknowledge the silence, give an easy out, leave the door open. | "want me to stop? just say so" |

## Rules that apply to every structure
- Open with the top `ranked_angles` hook (already ranked). One hook, not three.
- `proof_points` from the goal must each carry evidence; state one, not a wall.
- The CTA friction must be near zero at step 1 — a one-word reply or a single click, never a
  30-minute booking ask on a cold first touch.
- No fake urgency, no guarantees (`guardrails.banned_claims`), no `Re:`/`Fwd:` on step 1.
- Subject: specific and honest, tied to the hook or the value; not clickbait, not `Re:`.

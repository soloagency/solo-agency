# The Weave — the rhetorical engine (core skill)

This is the operator's proven method and the core value of the module. A cold message is not a
3-slot form (hook → value → CTA). It is a **ramp**: each public, professional signal is turned into
a conclusion, the conclusions build a tension, and the offer releases it. Length and depth **scale
with how much real material the dossier gives you** — brevity is not a universal law here.

Load this whenever you draft. `structures.md` maps the release to each `goal_type`; `channels.md`
maps the same weave onto email vs messenger.

---

## ANTI-CREEPY STANCE (the single most important tone rule — read first)

The writer is **a dedicated peer in the same service space who did their homework before a
professional approach** — NOT a surveillant. Concretely:
- Reference ONLY **public, professional** signals (Layer B). Personal-life details are already
  barred (`do_not_mention`); never surface them.
- Frame like a colleague's courtesy — "I looked at your page and one thing stood out…" — NOT a
  catalogue readout ("on July 3 you posted X, on July 8 you posted Y, you have 10,431 followers…").
- **Do NOT stack specifics to prove how much you researched** — that is exactly what reads as
  scraped/creepy. Use only the load-bearing points that each earn a conclusion; drop the rest.
- Never imply monitoring/persistence ("I've been following you", "over the past weeks I noticed").
- Public professional facts a peer would naturally notice, each doing rhetorical work, framed
  warmly and usefully = correct. An exhaustive, dated, itemized surveillance readout = wrong.

(This sharpens, not loosens, the existing `etiquette.md` rule "never stack details to look
impressive — it looks scraped.") The gold emails below reference several facts each — but every one
is a **beat in the arc**, never a readout. The count is fine; the *purpose* of each fact is the test.

---

## FACTS vs CONCLUSIONS (keeps the no-fabrication gate intact)

A **fact** must trace to a dossier hook with an `evidence_url` — it is what you literally observed
("your video has 52 shares"). A **conclusion** is the writer's honest INFERENCE from that fact
("people are already passing your version along") — stated as inference, never invented as a new
fact. You may reason freely from evidenced facts; you may never manufacture a fact. The evidence
gate is unchanged: only `contact.enrichment.hooks` facts may appear, passed as `hooks_used`.

## The four pillars

### Pillar 1 — Layer → Conclusion (never state a fact flat)
Each Layer-B fact is immediately turned into an implication that advances the goal.
- "77K and 76K views" → "the market has already shown it will stop for your content."
- "52 shares" → "people are not just seeing Scottsdale, they are passing your version of it along."
- "GL and workers-comp reels" → "you are already talking about the exact problems business owners
  ignore until a claim happens."

**The cut rule (this is the anti-list rule):** a fact that produces no conclusion advancing the goal
gets CUT. The discipline is not "use fewer facts" — it is "**every fact must do rhetorical work.**"
Three facts that each earn a conclusion beat ten facts read out like a scrape.

### Pillar 2 — Tension → Release (thắt–mở)
Build a tension, then release it — the offer is the resolution.
- **Thắt (tension):** name the cost of the current state. The signature move is *"that is
  expensive, because…"* — take something good (viral moments, real proof, talent on camera) and show
  why leaving it un-systematized quietly costs them. The tension is almost always the same shape:
  *you already have the proof/talent/moments, but they are one-off, not a system.*
- **Mở (release):** the offer (the 30-day content system) is the exact thing that resolves the
  tension you built. Release lands on the offer link, not on a generic ask.

The tension and the offer must be the same axis: if the tension is "scattered one-off moments," the
release must be "a steady system." A tension the offer does not resolve is a wasted paragraph.

### Pillar 3 — Arc → Goal (the whole message is one ramp)
The proven arc, in order. Every beat is optional-by-data (see the 3 modes) but the ORDER holds:

1. **Observation** — open on the *load-bearing* signal, reframed. Demote the vanity metric out
   loud when it helps ("the thing that jumped out is not the 710 followers — it is the Reels with
   77K views"). Lead with the asset or the buried asset, never the follower count for its own sake.
2. **Conclusion** — turn that observation into an implication (Pillar 1) so it means something.
3. **Reframe into the latent gap (Layer C, derived HERE)** — the tension. The good-but-scattered
   present state becomes a named, resolvable gap: "individual moments, not a system." Layer C is not
   in the dossier; you infer it from Layer-B facts + the offer, at write time.
4. **Defuse the likely objection** — pre-empt the reflex "no." Use Layer-B positioning/reputation
   facts to close the escape hatch: "you are licensed since 2006, verified, already have Reels —
   this is not a camera problem, it is a consistency problem." Turns "I can't / I don't need this"
   into "the only thing missing is the system."
5. **Anchor ROI to their real numbers** — the "one client" math, tied to *their* evidenced price /
   deal size: "your Zillow average is $655K… this does not have to bring ten clients to be worth it,
   it has to bring one." This defuses the price objection before it is spoken.
6. **Release into the offer** — the resolution (Pillar 2 mở): the specific proposal + link.
7. **Mechanism as friction-reducer** — show how little they must do: "we write the script and build
   the visuals; you record ~5 minutes off a teleprompter link; we publish to 10 platforms once you
   approve." Effort objection, pre-empted.
8. **Near-zero-friction CTA** — matched to `goal_type` (`structures.md`). "Worth a look?" not "book
   a 30-minute call."
9. **Signature + easy out** — name, brand, physical address (email; added by the send engine), and
   the honest opt-out ("reply 'no thanks' and I'll stop"). Packaging differs by channel
   (`channels.md`).

The ramp is a persuasion sequence, not a checklist to pad. Cut any beat the data cannot support
honestly — but keep the ORDER of the beats you do keep.

### Pillar 4 — Adapt to data richness (3 modes — length scales with material)
The number and strength of Layer-B points sets the mode. **This replaces the old "always 3–5
sentences" rule.** Brevity is a mode, not a law.

- **RICH (≥3 solid Layer-B points) → full weave.** Run the whole arc (beats 1–9), multiple
  fact→conclusion moves, the full tension. Advanced move for high-status leads (many points, big
  numbers): the *meta-open* — acknowledge they don't need you, then narrow the gap ("that is exactly
  why I almost did not send this — you already understand video better than almost anyone; the gap I
  noticed is narrower…"). See the Serhant reference below.
- **MEDIUM (1–2 solid points) → tight arc.** One strong observation → one reframe into the gap → one
  ROI anchor → offer. Skip the extra fact→conclusion loops; keep the tension and the "one client"
  math. Most leads land here.
- **THIN (1 weak point / fallback) → the short honest opener.** **THIS is the only place the old
  "3–5 sentences" guidance applies.** When enrich returns near-empty (a nearly blank page, no
  reviews), the *gap itself is the observation*: "your page is set up to be contacted — WhatsApp
  button, website — but it is nearly empty, so a prospect who finds you has no reason yet to trust
  you before clicking the next office." Honest, short, no manufactured hook. This mode still obeys
  the floor gate: if there is **zero** Layer-B signal, do not write — fall back per
  `no_hook_fallback` (default `skip`); never fabricate a hook to reach length.

Do not inflate a MEDIUM lead into a RICH email by padding, and do not compress a RICH lead into a
THIN one out of a misremembered "keep it short" rule. Match the mode to the material.

## Annotated gold references (the acceptance bar)

These are the operator's own hand-written emails. If this skill is followed, it should produce mail
of this caliber. Each is decomposed into the arc. (Bracketed labels = the pillar/beat, not part of

### Reference A — Angela Bommarito (real estate) · RICH mode · full weave

> Your Facebook page has 10K followers, a verified badge, and a visible video called "Scottsdale
> never disappoints!" that already has 52 shares.  `[1 Observation — leads with the asset]`
>
> That is a real asset. People are not just seeing Scottsdale, they are passing your version of it
> along.  `[2 Conclusion — "52 shares" → people are sharing it; Pillar 1]`  The expensive part is
> that those videos still feel like individual moments, not a steady content system that turns the
> Scottsdale lifestyle into repeat attention.  `[3 Reframe into Layer-C gap + Pillar-2 thắt: "the
> expensive part is…"]`
>
> Your site says you have been licensed since 2006 and work with primary homes, second homes, luxury
> condos, and investment properties. You are clearly not missing credibility.  `[4 Defuse objection
> — uses positioning facts to close the "am I credible enough" escape hatch]`  Your page already has
> Reels, and your video is out front, so this is not a camera problem. It is a consistency problem.
> `[4 cont. — sharpens the reframe: not camera, consistency]`
>
> A Scottsdale or Paradise Valley client does not need to find you ten times for this to be worth it.
> They need to remember you once when they are ready.  `[5 ROI anchor — the "one client" math,
> localized to her market]`
>
> I made you a 30-day content plan with 15 topics and the reason behind each one: [URL]  `[6 Release
> into the offer — Pillar-2 mở; the system resolves the "one-off moments" tension]`
>
> We write the script and build the visuals. You record about 5 minutes off a teleprompter link. We
> publish to 10 platforms once you approve.  `[7 Mechanism = friction reducer]`
>
> Worth a look?  `[8 Near-zero CTA — goal_type direct_sale/get_reply]`
> Binh Nguyen / LeadUp / [address] / Reply "no thanks" and I will stop.  `[9 Signature + easy out]`

**Why it works:** five facts (10K, verified, 52-share video, licensed-since-2006, four property
types) and not one is a readout — each is a beat: the video+shares establish the asset, the
license+property-types defuse the credibility objection. The tension ("individual moments, not a
system") is the *exact* axis the offer resolves.

## Quick self-check before you call `draft write`
- Does every fact earn a conclusion? If a sentence states a fact and stops, cut it or conclude it.
- Is there ONE tension, and does the offer resolve *that* tension (same axis)?
- Is the ROI anchored to the lead's own evidenced numbers, framed as "one client, not ten"?
- Did you demote vanity metrics and lead with the load-bearing signal?
- Does the mode (RICH/MEDIUM/THIN) match the dossier's Layer-B count — not a habit of length?
- Would a busy peer read this as a colleague's courtesy, or as a scrape? (Anti-creepy stance.)
- Every referenced fact is in `hooks_used` with an `evidence_url`; nothing in `do_not_mention`.

When any file disagrees with `docs/DESIGN.md`, `docs/DESIGN.md` wins.

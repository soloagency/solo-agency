# Channels — one weave, two wrappers (email vs messenger/DM)

The persuasion is channel-agnostic. **The weave (`weave.md`) — the arc, the four pillars, the
fact→conclusion moves, the tension→release — is identical across channels.** Only the *packaging*
changes: subject, length, rhythm, opener, and footer. Do not rebuild the argument per channel; build
it once, then wrap it for the channel the dossier's Layer-A reachability gives you.

The hard gates (evidence_url per fact, `do_not_mention`, no fabrication, draft-never-sends, anti-
creepy stance) apply on **every** channel without exception.

---

## What is SHARED (never changes by channel)
- The arc: observation → conclusion → reframe into the Layer-C gap → defuse objection → ROI anchor →
  release into offer → mechanism → near-zero CTA.
- The four pillars and the "every fact earns a conclusion" cut rule.
- The data-richness mode (RICH / MEDIUM / THIN) — set by Layer-B count, not by channel.
- The anti-creepy stance: a peer who did their homework, never a surveillant.
- Facts vs conclusions, and the evidence gate.

## What CHANGES by channel (packaging only)

| Element | EMAIL | MESSENGER / DM |
|---|---|---|
| Subject line | Required. Honest + specific, tied to the hook/value ("your Scottsdale videos, Angela"). Never `Re:`/`Fwd:` on step 1. | **None** — DMs have no subject. Fold the hook into the first line instead. |
| Opener | Can name the discovery act: "I opened your Facebook Reels and…". | You are **already on their profile** — "I opened your Facebook…" is redundant/odd. Open straight on the observation: "Your Reels with 77K views caught me — the market clearly stops for your content." |
| Length | A bit more room; the full weave can breathe (esp. RICH mode). | **Shorter.** Tighten every beat; often merge mechanism + CTA into one line. Compress, don't drop beats. |
| Rhythm | Written paragraphs. | More conversational, shorter sentences, more line breaks; reads like a message a peer would actually send. |
| Signature | Name + brand + **physical address** + opt-out. The physical address + opt-out footer is CAN-SPAM and is added by the send engine's MIME + footer — you write the sign-off, not the legal footer. | Name + brand only. **No physical-address footer** (not an email; CAN-SPAM's postal-address rule does not apply). Keep a light opt-out in your own words ("reply 'no' and I'll stop"). |
| CTA | "Worth a look?" + the proposal link. | Same intent, even lower friction; a link is fine but keep it to one, conversational ("want me to send the 30-day plan?"). |
| Compliance footer | Physical address + unsubscribe, appended by the send engine (Stage 8) — do not hand-type it. | No postal footer; honest easy-out only. |

## The opener, concretely
- **Email** (from the gold set): *"I opened your Facebook Reels and the thing that jumped out is not
  the 710 followers. It is the Reels with 77K and 76K views."* — naming the discovery act is natural
  because you arrived from outside.
- **DM**: you are *inside* their profile already, so drop the "I opened your Facebook" frame and lead
  with the reframed observation itself: *"Your Reels doing 77K/76K views — not the 710 followers —
  are the real signal here. The market already stops for your content; it's just not compounding into
  an audience you own yet."* Same Pillar-1 move (demote vanity metric → conclusion), one wrapper
  lighter.

## Length by channel × mode
The mode still comes from the data (`weave.md`); the channel just sets the ceiling.
- Email · RICH → full arc, room to run.  Email · MEDIUM → tight arc.  Email · THIN → short honest opener.
- DM · RICH → the full arc, compressed into short lines (merge mechanism+CTA).  DM · MEDIUM → 3–5
  short lines.  DM · THIN → two lines: the honest observation + the ask.
- **Compress by tightening, not by deleting the tension or the ROI anchor** — those two beats carry
  the persuasion. A DM that keeps only "hook + link" is a flyer, not a weave.

## Assisted / phone-adjacent touches
When Layer-A reachability is only a phone or a "message" button (no email, no open DM), the same arc
becomes talking points for an assisted/human touch: the observation → the one-client ROI → the offer.
Keep it a peer's courtesy, not a script read at someone.

## Note: the Vietnamese gold set corroborates this
Several of the operator's gold emails are written in Vietnamese to VN-market attorneys, in a warmer,
more conversational register (closer to the DM wrapper). They keep the **same arc** — proof/asset →
the gap ("proof chưa được đóng gói thành chuỗi video đều đặn" = "the proof isn't packaged into a
steady video series") → the LeadUp mechanism → the easy out ("reply 'không' là em ngưng"). The weave
survives both translation and a more casual channel: further evidence that the argument is the
constant and the wrapper is the variable.
